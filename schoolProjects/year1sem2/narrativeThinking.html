Narrative Thinking

Soft skills

This module has taught me how to analyze narratives. There are narratives everywhere like videos, songs, movies and advertisements. For this project, it is aplit into two. The first part having to select a narrative that has impacted me personally and analyse it accordingly using the techniques I've learnt in class. Additionally, we have to present our finding to the class. The second part being having to create my own narrative.

Part 1.

Working in pairs, my partner and I selected a narrative. After careful considerations, we choose a song narrative. The song is called 'when the party's over' by Billie Eilish

Firstly, we watched through the music video and read the lyrics to get to know about the narrative. We then summarised it.

Knowing more about the narrative, we are then able to identify the target audience for this narrative which is teenagers. There are several reasons for my partner and I in choosing this narrative. Mostly because it left an impactful impression on us.

When analysing the song, we took note of not only the lyrics but also the music video itself. The way Billie Eilish presents herself in the music video also plays a part in narrating the story. With that in mind, we picked out three key messages from the narrative and how we derived them.

From the key messages, we recognized the techniques use to articulate these messages. The techniques used are emotional appeal, associations, need & desires.

Lastly after discovering the techniques used, we investigated the effectiveness of it. We researched more on the video like the number of views it gets and the comment left by people. The comments shows how the video has impacted on them, so it shows how effective the narrative was in conveying its message to the audience.

Part 2.

For this part of the project, it is an individual work to craft a personal real-life scenario narrative. The narrative has to relevance or importantance and able to persuade the target audience to undertake a course of action.

I decided to craft a narrative on mental health as I think it is a topic that not many people have the awareness about and to try and get rid the stigma around it. Firstly, I had to do research on mental health. As mental health is very broad, I focused on self-harming. I identified my target audience, purpose of narrative and the context of where, when the narrative will be told, organiser and theme.

While writing the narrative, I kept in mind of how a narrative structure would go (trigger event, transformation, life lesson, call to action). Besides that, I added in my research findings into the narrative where necessary. To spice up the narrative, I included rhetorical appeals (pathos, logos, ethos) to engage the audience and effective signposting to guide audience through the narrative.

Once I finalised the script for the narrative that I would be presenting to the class, I preacticed on how the delivery of the narrative would go. In the script, the brackets is to indicate non-verbal actions like pausing, emphasizing on certain words and showing a certain item.
